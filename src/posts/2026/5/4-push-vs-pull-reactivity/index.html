TODO: intro. The one-sentence hook: when a value changes, does the source shove the update out to whoever cares, or do consumers come ask for it when they need it? People call this "push vs. pull," and signals get called "push-pull," and somehow almost every framework ends up sounding like it's both. That's because the term quietly bundles two different questions together.

A note before we start: there's a related question this post deliberately does not cover- when the work actually runs (this frame, next microtask, on idle). That's scheduling, and it's its own axis. We'll get to it in Part 7. Here we only care about whether a notification is sent and when the value is resolved- not the timing of the flush.

Push/pull is really two questions

TODO: the key move of the whole post. "Push/pull" overloads two separate questions, and untangling them is what makes everything click:

  • Q1 - Notification. On a write, does the system actively reach out and tell its dependents "you're dirty"? Or does it tell nobody, and let readers discover the change on their own?
  • Q2 - Resolution. Is the new value computed eagerly, right at write time? Or lazily, the next time something reads it?

TODO: signals are the canonical "push-pull" precisely because they answer these two questions differently: push the notification (Q1), pull the value (Q2). That combination is the thing people are gesturing at when they say a signal is push-pull.

The 2×2 (and why it collapses to three buckets)

TODO: lay out the grid. Q1 (notify / no-notify) on one side, Q2 (eager / lazy) on the other. Four cells, but one is degenerate: no-notify + eager resolve is incoherent- eager computation needs something to fire it, and that firing is a notification. So it's only reachable when there are no dependents, i.e. nothing reactive to plot. Three reachable strategies fall out:

  • Pull (no notify + lazy): the write tells no dependents; readers discover changes by re-running or polling. React, Redux, Lit, AngularJS, and- the surprising ones- Ember and Angular Zone.js.
  • Push-pull (notify + lazy): the write actively wakes its dependents, but values recompute lazily on read. The whole signals family lives here: Solid, Preact signals, Vue, Svelte 5, MobX, Valtio, nanostores, Angular Signals.
  • Push (notify + eager): the write computes and applies the update immediately, with no lazy read phase. Compiled Svelte 4, event buses, Cycle.js, RxJS.

TODO: note this is the same three buckets shown on the radar in Part 3, and the spectrum on the chart in Part 1- the color key everywhere is pull / push-pull / push.

The surprising pulls: Ember and Angular Zone

TODO: this is the part worth dwelling on, because it's counterintuitive. Ember's Glimmer is fine-grained and feels reactive, but a @tracked write only bumps a global revision clock- it notifies no dependents. The render pass later compares clocks and recomputes what's stale. Invalidation is not a notification. A clock bump, a dirty flag, a setState schedule, a zone tick- none of these compute or notify; they just mark or schedule. So they're all pull. Angular Zone is the same: the tick says "something happened, recheck everything," carries no dependency information, and the dirty-check resolves at render. Pull, same bucket as React- despite the very different machinery.

Push: the source tells you

TODO: the pure-push end. Event buses, observable streams, compiled Svelte 4. The write both notifies and resolves on the spot- a listener fires, a stream emits a value downstream, a compiled assignment writes straight to the DOM. No deferred read phase. Use the imperative/implicit-signaling material in the draft comment above to ground "how the notification gets sent."

Pull: the consumer asks

TODO: the pure-pull end. React's render cycle, the digest loop, the game loop (forward-reference Part 7). Nobody is notified; the consumer re-reads on its own schedule and figures out what changed- by diffing (React), by polling watchers (AngularJS), by comparing revision clocks (Ember). The defining trait: the write left no breadcrumb pointing at a dependent.

Push-pull: notify, then resolve lazily

TODO: the middle, where the signals family lives and where most modern reactivity has converged. The write pushes a precise notification to exactly the dependents that read the value (push), but the new value isn't computed until something reads it (pull). Walk Solid (wake the effect, lazy memo), Vue (wake the render effect, lazy re-run + diff), MobX. Tie back to the diamond problem from Part 3: resolving lazily on read is part of how these systems stay glitch-free- you mark dirty eagerly but recompute in dependency order only when the value is actually pulled.
