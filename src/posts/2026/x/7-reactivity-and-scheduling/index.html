The simplest scheduler: the game loop

TODO: the classic while (running) { update(); render(); } loop. No notifications, no change detection- it just pulls the entire world and redraws every frame, on a fixed clock. It is the purest pull there is, and the simplest possible schedule: do everything, every frame, forever. AngularJS's digest cycle is the same animal for the DOM.

Why frameworks can't just do that

TODO: re-rendering everything every frame is too expensive for a real app, so frameworks defer and coalesce the work instead. This is where scheduling strategies diverge: microtask batching, requestAnimationFrame, idle callbacks, time-slicing.

Scheduling is orthogonal to push/pull

TODO: two frameworks can sit in the same push/pull bucket and schedule completely differently. A game loop and React are both pull, but one runs on a fixed tick and the other coalesces work on demand after a setState. Note: this is where the Angular zone "tick" actually belongs- it's a scheduling decision (when to run change detection), not a push/pull one.
